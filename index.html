📜 Case File 1 — The Twin Unknowns

CASE #001   Two missing numbers. Two clues. Find them both.

The Big Idea

A simultaneous equation is when you have two mystery numbers (let's call them x and y) and two different clues about them. One clue alone isn't enough — but together, the two clues lock down the answer like a pair of fingerprints.

The Detective's Rule

Two unknowns? You need two clues.
Three unknowns? You'd need three clues. And so on!

Picture each equation as an old-fashioned set of scales. Whatever's on the left equals whatever's on the right — they're balanced.

Two apples and a banana cost £7. One apple and two bananas cost £8. What's the price of each?

Method 1 — The "Line Up the Suspects" Method (Elimination)

Sometimes you can cancel out one of the unknowns by adding or subtracting the two equations. It's like ruling out a suspect.

2x + y = 7   ← Clue A
 x + 2y = 8   ← Clue B x = price of one apple, y = price of one banana
Step 1. Multiply Clue B by 2 so the x's match:   2x + 4y = 16
Step 2. Subtract Clue A from this new equation:   (2x + 4y) − (2x + y) = 16 − 7
Step 3. The x's cancel!   3y = 9   →   y = 3
Step 4. Put y back into Clue A:   2x + 3 = 7   →   x = 2
Apple = £2, Banana = £3. Check: 2(£2) + £3 = £7 ✓   £2 + 2(£3) = £8 ✓
Case closed!

Method 2 — The "Disguise" Method (Substitution)

Rearrange one clue to say "x equals…" and then sneak that disguise into the other clue.

y = x + 1   ← Clue A (already disguised)
2x + y = 10   ← Clue B
Step 1. Replace y in Clue B with (x + 1):   2x + (x + 1) = 10
Step 2. Tidy up:   3x + 1 = 10   →   3x = 9   →   x = 3
Step 3. Put x = 3 back in Clue A:   y = 3 + 1 = 4
x = 3, y = 4. Two unknowns, two clues, two answers. The trail led right to them.

🗺️ Mystery Puzzle: The Pirate's Treasure Chest

EVIDENCE

Captain Blackbeak's Loot

You've cracked into the captain's logbook. It says:

🗝️🗝️🗝️ + 💎💎 = 19 doubloons
🗝️ + 💎💎💎 = 18 doubloons

Each 🗝️ key is worth the same. Each 💎 gem is worth the same. Find the value of each!

Let k = key, g = gem. Then: 3k + 2g = 19   and   k + 3g = 18.
Multiply the second by 3: 3k + 9g = 54. Subtract the first: 7g = 35, so g = 5. Then k = 18 − 15 = 3.
Key = 3 doubloons, Gem = 5 doubloons.

🐉 Mystery Puzzle: The Dragon's Riddle

CLUE #2

The Cave of Two Numbers

A dragon blocks your path. He growls:

"I am thinking of two numbers. Their sum is 14. Their difference is 4. Tell me both, and you may pass."

Let the numbers be x and y. x + y = 14, x − y = 4.
Add the two equations: 2x = 18 → x = 9. Then y = 14 − 9 = 5.
The numbers are 9 and 5. The dragon steps aside.

🌀 Case File 2 — The Two-Faced Number

CASE #002   One mystery number… that may have two identities.

The Big Idea

A quadratic equation is one that contains a squared mystery number, like x². The shocking twist? These equations often have two correct answers — like a suspect with a twin!

x² − 5x + 6 = 0 The classic shape: ax² + bx + c = 0

Visual: The Smile (and the Frown)

If you draw a quadratic on a graph, it makes a U-shape called a parabola. The places it crosses the x-axis are the answers — and there can be two!

Two crossings = two answers. Both are right!

Method: Factorising (a.k.a. "Splitting the Suspects")

The trick is to rewrite x² + bx + c as (x + ?)(x + ?). You're looking for two numbers that multiply to give c and add to give b.

x² − 5x + 6 = 0 Need two numbers: multiply to +6, add to −5

The Suspect Line-Up

List pairs of numbers that multiply to +6, then check which pair adds to −5:

Suspect PairMultiplyAddVerdict
1 and 66 ✓7❌
2 and 36 ✓5❌
−1 and −66 ✓−7❌
−2 and −36 ✓−5✅ GUILTY
So:   x² − 5x + 6 = (x − 2)(x − 3) = 0
The Big Idea: If two things multiply to give zero, one of them must be zero. So either x − 2 = 0 (giving x = 2) or x − 3 = 0 (giving x = 3).
x = 2 OR x = 3. Both fit! Try them: (2)² − 5(2) + 6 = 0 ✓ and (3)² − 5(3) + 6 = 0 ✓
The Detective's Trick: Look at the signs. If c is positive and b is negative, both your suspects are negative. If c is negative, one suspect is positive and one is negative.

🏰 Mystery Puzzle: The Garden of Secrets

CASE FILE

The Lady's Rectangular Garden

An old letter says the lady's secret garden has an area of 12 square metres. The length is 1 metre longer than the width. What are the dimensions?

Setting It Up

Let width = x. Then length = x + 1.
Area = length × width = x(x + 1) = 12
Expand: x² + x − 12 = 0

Find two numbers that multiply to −12 and add to +1.

The pair is +4 and −3 (multiply: −12 ✓, add: +1 ✓).
So (x + 4)(x − 3) = 0 → x = −4 or x = 3.
A garden can't have negative width, so width = 3 m, length = 4 m. Quadratics often give two answers, but real life sometimes only allows one of them — that's part of the mystery!

🦇 Mystery Puzzle: The Bat Cave

URGENT

The Echoing Equation

Carved on the cave wall in glowing letters:

x² − 7x + 10 = 0

The cave only opens when you whisper both values of x.

Two numbers that multiply to +10 and add to −7? −2 and −5.
So (x − 2)(x − 5) = 0 → x = 2 or x = 5. The cave creaks open!

🎓 Detective's Cheat Sheet

Simultaneous Equations

What it is: Two unknowns, two clues. Both must be true at the same time.
Best moves:
  • Elimination: add or subtract the equations to make one variable disappear.
  • Substitution: rearrange one equation to "x = …" and slot it into the other.

Quadratic Equations

What it is: Has an x² in it. Often has two answers.
Best move (for now): Factorise. Find two numbers that multiply to c and add to b. Then each bracket = 0 gives an answer.
Always check your answer! Plug it back into the original equation. If it works, you cracked the case. If not, the trail's gone cold — go back and look for the slip.
